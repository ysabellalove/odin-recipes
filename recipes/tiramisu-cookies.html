Tiramisu Cookies

Description

This tiramisu cookie recipe takes all the classic flavour elements of a rich and creamy tiramisu and turns it into a cookie. Even better, this recipe is no chill and can be made in one bowl!

Ingredients

Equipment

  • 2 mixing bowls
  • 1 medium cookie scoop (2tbsp)
  • 2 baking sheets
  • 2 sheets parchment paper
  • 1 stand mixer with whisk attachment
  • 1 icing spatula or spoon
  • 1 small mesh strainer for dusting cocoa powder

Coffee Sugar Cookies

  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp instant powder espresso
  • 2 1/2 cups all purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, room temp
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Espresso Mascarpone Frosting

  • 8 oz. mascarpone cheese, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp instant powder espresso
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup cold heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder for dusting

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, melt the butter and whisk in the espresso powder. Transfer to the fridge and cool for about 10 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk in the cooled espresso butter with the granulated sugar until well combined. Whisk in the egg and vanilla extract until smooth and well combined. Stir the dry ingredients into the wet until just combined. I like to do this about half of the dry ingredients at a time.
  4. Preheat the oven to 375F for about 20-30 minutes before baking the cookies. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper. Allow the dough to rest while the oven preheats. Using a medium cookie scoop, scoop the cookie dough and place the cookie dough balls onto the lined baking sheet about 3 inches apart.
  5. Bake at 375F for 10-12 minutes or until the edges of the cookies are set and the centers are slightly puffed.
  6. Cool the chewy coffee sugar cookies on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. Then transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely to room temperature. Make the mascarpone frosting while the cookies cool.
  7. On low, whisk the mascarpone cheese until smooth. Once smooth, mix in the espresso powder, vanilla extract, and powdered sugar until well combined.
  8. Once all the sugar has been added, mix in the heavy cream slowly on low speed. Once all the heavy cream has been added, increase the mixer speed to medium for about 2-3 minutes or until the frosting is light and fluffy.
